## Refuse, not attact

I try to learn how to gentaly refuse some invitations. Refusal does not mean to attack at all. In my old thinking model, refusal equaled attack. The reason? I used to imagine refusal meant neglect, and my father's neglect hurt me so bad when I was 12. He said you were on you own now. I was like a small boat. There were no sign of lighthouses, harbors nearby, and there were no tools in my boat. In my view,  his move is beyond a mere attact. However, in this moment, the equation has collapsed."I'll pass this time." and "I'll take the rain check." are good way to refuse an invitation without any harm and the best thing is they both leave room for next connection. 
Keep sailing. Captain!
